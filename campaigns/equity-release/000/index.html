What is Equity release?

A product that enables older people (over 55s) to raise capital or income from their property while continuing to occupy their homes for the rest of their lives or until they go into full time care.

What is Equity?

This is the residual wealth of a property owner that is tied up in their property. So the market value less any monies due to others secured on it.

What products are there?

Lifetime Mortgage:

A Lifetime Mortgage which is like other mortgages except it has an open ended term (It is redeemed on death or entry into full time care which is an unknown date) The mortgage is interest only which can be paid monthly or rolled up and paid at the end of the term from the sale of the property.

Home Reversion:

Home Reversion is where the owner sells all or part of the property to a Reversion Provider and remains in the property as a tenant for life. The sale will be for a percentage of the market value in exchange for the right to reside.

Who regulates Equity release and what safeguards are there?

Equity release products are regulated by the FCA. they have a no negative equity guarantee and are protected by a body called The equity Release Council which was established on 28/05/2012. The council’s mission is “working to bring outcomes that are in the best interest of customers.”

Why Release Equity?

There are a number of reasons why people may wish to do this, here are a few:

1. Pay off an existing mortgage, loan or debts secured on the property.
2. To generate income to supplement pension/retirement income.
3. To raise capital to go on holidays, purchase a new car, home improvements etc.
4. Inheritance tax mitigation. Benefitting from funds now and reducing inheritance tax bill for family in the future by decreasing the residual value of an estate.
